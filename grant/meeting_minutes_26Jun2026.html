Agenda Item 3
Project Rollout Plan — If Funding is Successful
Mr. Christopher Kenia (Field Operations Manager) led the discussion on the proposed rollout strategy. The team agreed on a phased approach over the 12-month project period (1 August 2026 – 31 July 2027):
  • Month 1–2 (August–September 2026) — Setup & Preparation: Finalise field onboarding materials, training guides, and demo devices. Identify target markets across NCD. Ms. Mirriam Jack to lead community engagement and prior communications to market associations.
  • Month 3–5 (October–December 2026) — Pilot Rollout: Field agents (Leonard Taman, Joshua Ivan, Micha Gau) to conduct on-site vendor onboarding at three initial NCD market locations. Target minimum 50 vendors registered and active on CatalogKit by end of Month 5.
  • Month 6–9 (January–April 2027) — Full Rollout & Scale: Expand to additional NCD markets. Community training workshops to be conducted fortnightly. Ms. Jack to coordinate WhatsApp group support channels for registered vendors.
  • Month 10–12 (May–July 2027) — Review & Reporting: Data collection, impact assessment, and final grant report preparation. Ms. Annrika Garena to compile financial acquittal report. Ms. Yurus to prepare technical summary report for NCDC.
Agenda Item 4
Testing & Quality Assurance Plan
Mr. Isaac Matava (Web Developer) presented the testing framework for ensuring CatalogKit performs reliably for non-tech-savvy vendors in low-connectivity environments:
  • User Acceptance Testing (UAT): A structured UAT session will be conducted with 10–15 selected market vendors prior to the pilot rollout. Participants will include individuals with no prior digital catalog experience. Feedback will directly inform any final platform adjustments before full deployment.
  • Connectivity Testing: The platform will be tested on 3G and limited-data mobile connections — the primary connectivity scenario for PNG market vendors — to ensure image upload, catalog generation, and WhatsApp sharing all function reliably under these conditions.
  • Device Compatibility: Testing to cover a range of low-to-mid-range Android smartphones commonly used by vendors in NCD markets.
  • Field Agent Feedback Loop: Field agents to report platform issues weekly during the pilot phase. Mr. Matava will triage and resolve reported bugs within 48 hours where possible.
  • WhatsApp Sharing Verification: Each catalog generated during pilot onboarding will be shared via WhatsApp by the vendor and confirmed to render correctly on the recipient's device.

The team agreed that a simple paper feedback form will also be used during field sessions for vendors who are uncomfortable with digital surveys.
