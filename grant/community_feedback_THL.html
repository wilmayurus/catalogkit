Community Feedback & Tester Testimonials

CatalogKit  ·  Trey Holdings Limited  ·  June 2026
Community Support Evidence — Innovation Grant Application
This document presents written feedback collected from community members and early testers of the CatalogKit platform. Testers were selected from NCD market vendor communities — particularly women vendors and small business owners with little to no prior experience with digital tools. Their responses demonstrate genuine grassroots demand for the platform and directly support the community benefit criteria of the NCDC Innovation Grant.

Tester Testimonials
"
I am not good with phones and computers — I always ask my children to help me. But when Wilma showed me this CatalogKit, I could see my products in the catalog on the screen and I was very happy. I sell fresh food and second-hand clothes at the market. Before, I would just show my things to people who walk past. Now I can send a link on WhatsApp and people can see everything I sell without coming to the market. It will really help me reach more customers. I did not think something like this could be for people like me — for free. I support this project fully.
VO
Victoria Oksen
Market Vendor — Fresh Produce & Second-hand Goods, NCD
Widow Non-tech-savvy Community Tester WhatsApp User
"
I have been selling at the market for many years. I have seven children and I depend on my market sales to pay school fees. When they showed me how to use CatalogKit, I put in my products and it made a nice booklet — like a real shop brochure. I shared it with my church group on WhatsApp and three people asked me about my goods the same day. That has never happened to me before. I do not know much about technology but this was easy — I just followed the steps. If the government can support this kind of tool for us market mamas, it will change our lives. God bless this project.
JB
Jacinta Bera
Market Vendor — Cooked Food & Household Goods, NCD
Widow Non-tech-savvy Community Tester WhatsApp User
Note on testing method: Feedback was gathered through in-person demonstration sessions conducted by the CatalogKit field team. Each tester was shown the platform on a smartphone, assisted in creating a sample catalog, and asked to share their experience in their own words. Responses were recorded and transcribed verbatim. Both testers confirmed they are comfortable with their names and feedback being included in this grant application.
