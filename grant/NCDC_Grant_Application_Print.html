Section 3 — Project Description
Brief Summary of the Project
CatalogKit is a free, mobile-first web application developed by Trey Holdings Limited that enables PNG market vendors, informal traders, and small business operators to create professional digital product catalogs — without any technical knowledge or cost. A vendor photographs their products using their phone, uploads the images, and CatalogKit instantly generates a shareable flipbook catalog and a downloadable PDF ready to send on WhatsApp.

The platform is purpose-built for Papua New Guinea: it operates on slow 3G mobile data, supports local PNG payment methods (BSP, Kina, Westpac, Wantok Wallet, Pei), lists NCD market locations, and uses WhatsApp-first sharing because that is how business is done in PNG. The app is live at www.catalogkit.org and available free to all PNG users permanently.

To ensure no vendor is left behind, CatalogKit includes a Done-For-You Setup Service where trained agents visit market stalls and build the vendor's first catalog alongside them for K50 cash on the day — making it accessible even to non-literate and non-tech-savvy vendors.

Grant funds will support agent training, field operations, community outreach across NCD markets, and scaling the platform to serve at least 200 vendors in Year 1.
Objectives and Expected Outcomes
  1. Enable digital product showcasing — Allow informal market vendors and SMEs to present their products professionally online without any technical skills or financial cost.
  2. Increase sales and customer reach — Connect vendors' products to customers via WhatsApp and Facebook sharing, expanding reach beyond the physical market.
  3. Empower women entrepreneurs — Market mamas (the majority of informal vendors in PNG) gain free, accessible digital business tools specifically designed for them.
  4. Build digital literacy — The Done-For-You agent program ensures vendors learn digital skills through hands-on, side-by-side support at their own stalls.
  5. Demonstrate a replicable model — Establish a sustainable, community-led model for digital inclusion of PNG's informal sector that can expand beyond NCD.
Expected Outcomes by Month 12:
  • 200+ vendors onboarded with active digital catalogs
  • 60%+ of beneficiaries are women
  • 50+ Done-For-You agent visits completed
  • K50 agent fee model operational and self-sustaining
  • Platform documented and replication guide prepared for other provinces

Community Involvement & Gender Inclusion
The primary beneficiaries of CatalogKit are women market vendors — the market mamas who sustain PNG's informal economy at Gordons, Koki, Boroko, Waigani, and Gerehu markets. Women make up an estimated 70–80% of informal market traders in NCD and are the primary target of this project.

CatalogKit was designed from the ground up for gender inclusion. The interface requires no reading of complex instructions, uses visual step-by-step photo guidance, and operates entirely in a familiar WhatsApp-style workflow. The Done-For-You agent service ensures that older vendors, those with low literacy, and those unfamiliar with smartphones are not excluded — agents visit stalls, sit alongside vendors, and build their first catalog together.

Community involvement will be strengthened through:
  • Partnerships with existing market vendor associations at NCD markets
  • Word-of-mouth referral incentives (existing vendors invite fellow vendors)
  • Community demonstration sessions at markets (free 30-minute group sessions)
  • Women's group outreach via church networks and ward-level community groups

Grant funds will specifically prioritize women's market groups and cooperatives for first outreach, with a commitment that at least 60% of Year 1 beneficiaries are women.

Sustainability Plan
Financial sustainability: CatalogKit is built on free and low-cost cloud infrastructure, keeping ongoing operational costs below K400 per month. The K50 Done-For-You setup fee provides a self-funding mechanism for the agent program once the initial cohort is trained. The platform itself will remain permanently free to all vendors.

Community sustainability: Vendors who successfully use CatalogKit naturally introduce it to neighbouring stalls and wantoks. This organic word-of-mouth growth, combined with trained community agents already embedded in markets, creates a self-sustaining referral network that does not depend on ongoing grant funding.

Technical sustainability: Trey Holdings Limited maintains the platform as part of its ICT service portfolio. The system is built on industry-standard open-source tools (Python, PostgreSQL, Supabase) that can be maintained by any qualified PNG developer, reducing vendor lock-in. Full source code is retained by Trey Holdings Limited.

Scale-up pathway: Following successful Year 1 results in NCD, the model can be replicated in Lae, Madang, and Mt Hagen with minimal additional development cost — only agent training and outreach costs change. This provides a strong argument for future grant rounds or development partner funding.
